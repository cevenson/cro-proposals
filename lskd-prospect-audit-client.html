Full Funnel Review
What We Found Across the Four Conversion Pages
Home Page
  • MedHero tagline "alldayactive" is a lifestyle statement, not a conversion hook — new visitors can't immediately identify the brand's core offer.
  • MedAnnouncement bar promotes 15% off first app purchase — actively diverts web traffic toward the app before a web conversion attempt.
  • LowHero CTA ("Shop Now") appears slightly below fold on standard viewports — value prop and CTA aren't co-located above the scroll line.
  • LowNo trust signals above fold: no aggregate review count, press mentions, or star rating to anchor first impressions.
  • Strong editorial imagery; brand identity is visually distinct
  • Category navigation (Popular, Womens, Mens, Unisex) is clear
  • Community and Rewards links visible in header — loyalty signal
Product Listing Page
  • HighProduct cards display zero review indicators — star ratings and review counts are completely absent from PLP cards, despite flagship products having 10,000+ verified reviews.
  • Med432 results in the main Womens collection with no default sort by popularity or bestseller — high-converting products may be buried.
  • LowFilter options require tapping "Filter & Sort" — filter chips aren't pre-surfaced on desktop for a 432-result catalogue.
  • "Popular Style 🔥" and "New" badges provide browsing cues
  • "2 For $110" bundle badges surface offers at browse stage
  • Subcategory chip filters (Flare, 7/8, Bike Shorts, etc.) present
  • Quick-add (+) button on each card reduces friction
  • Core Styles editorial guide (Fusion/Rep/Elixir) aids decision
Product Detail Page
  • High"Find In-Store" CTA renders as a prominent full-width button directly above the size selector — the first actionable element an online shopper encounters creates an unexpected in-store off-ramp.
  • MedNo delivery estimate or shipping promise visible near the ATC zone — customers complete size selection without knowing when their order arrives or what the returns policy is.
  • MedNo per-size stock availability indicators — shoppers selecting a size have no urgency signal or awareness of low availability.
  • LowProduct Details and Model Details accordions are collapsed by default — key fit information requires an extra click to surface.
  • Sticky ATC bar ensures "Add to Bag" is always accessible
  • 4.7★ / 12,094 reviews visible inline — exceptional social proof
  • "Best-Seller" and "94%+ Recommended" image badges are strong
  • "Complete the Fit" cross-sell section exists after ATC
  • Fit indicator (Runs Small → True to Size → Runs Large) present
Cart / Checkout
  • MedCart drawer could not be fully evaluated in this audit — detailed assessment of trust signals, upsell placement, and checkout CTA requires analytics access.
  • LowEmpty cart page offers category navigation but no "recently viewed" or personalised product recovery prompt.
  • Cart is a slide-out drawer (no full-page redirect) — lower friction UX
  • Checkout is out of scope for Stage 1 per brief constraints
  • Cart page design is clean with clear navigation fallback

Risk Assessment
Risks & Caveats
⚠️
No analytics baseline — projections are relative only
All CVR lift estimates are based on industry benchmarks, not LSKD's actual traffic or conversion data. A store with 1% baseline CVR and one with 4% CVR will see very different absolute revenue impacts from the same relative lift.
Mitigation: Connect GA4 or share Shopify analytics for Stage 2 modelling.
📱
Mobile behaviour needs separate validation
This audit was conducted on a desktop viewport. Mobile represents the majority of sportswear e-commerce traffic. The sticky ATC, PLP card layout, and size selector UX should be separately validated on mobile before shipping variants.
Mitigation: Stage 2 includes a dedicated mobile crawl and Clarity/heatmap review.
🏬
Find In-Store relocation may reduce in-store traffic attribution
If LSKD's omnichannel strategy relies on the PDP as a store finder entry point, relocating this button may reduce in-store visit attribution. This is a business tradeoff, not just a UX decision.
Mitigation: Test the repositioned version first. Monitor in-store traffic via Store Locator page views as a secondary metric during the experiment.
📊
Test duration requires sufficient traffic volume
A/B tests on individual funnel pages require sufficient traffic to reach statistical significance. Without knowing LSKD's monthly sessions, we cannot estimate how long each test needs to run before a decision can be made (typically 2–4 weeks minimum).
Mitigation: Share monthly session volume at onboarding. We will calculate required sample size per test before launch.
