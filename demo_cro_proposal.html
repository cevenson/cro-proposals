Lumina Skincare — Routine & Replenish CRO Proposal

A data-backed proposal to increase AOV through routine building and stock-up incentives, based on analysis of 8,421 Shopify orders, 60 days of GA4 behavioral data, and qualitative insights from NotebookLM research.

What Your Customers Are Already Doing

We analyzed Lumina's recent transaction and session data. We also cross-referenced these quantitative findings with qualitative research stored in your NotebookLM workspace (customer interviews and post-purchase surveys). Here is what the data tells us:

The Double-Cleanse Behavior is Ubiquitous

Data: In the last 60 days, 1,205 sessions added both the "Gentle Foaming Cleanser" and the "Oil Makeup Melting Balm" to their cart. 850 completed the purchase (70% conversion rate).

NotebookLM Insight: "Customers frequently mention in reviews that they consider these two products a single 'step' in their evening routine. However, several users complained about having to navigate to two separate pages to buy them."

Sunscreen is a High-Volume Necessity

Data: 25% of all orders containing the "Daily Defense SPF 50" contain 2 or more units without any current volume discount.

NotebookLM Insight: "Post-purchase surveys show that 40% of SPF buyers are purchasing for multiple family members or stocking up for the summer."

Proposed Tests

Test 1: The "Complete Your Routine" Add-On

Target: Product pages for the Foaming Cleanser and Vitamin C Serum.

Mechanic: Offer a 15% discount on the complementary product (Oil Balm or SPF) when added directly from the current product page via a 1-click CTA.

Hypothesis: If we reduce the friction of finding the complementary product and provide a small financial incentive, AOV will increase because the co-viewing gap shows high underlying intent that is currently dropping off.

Test 2: SPF Stock-Up Tiers

Target: Daily Defense SPF 50 Product Page.

Mechanic: Tiered discounts: Buy 2 get 10% off the second, Buy 3 get 15% off the third+.

Hypothesis: If we incrementally reward bulk purchases on high-replenishment items, units per order will increase because NotebookLM survey data confirms users already buy this for multiple family members.
