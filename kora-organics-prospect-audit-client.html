Category Research · NotebookLM
What Skincare Shoppers Tell Us They Need
Social proof unlocks the first purchase
Skincare has one of the highest first-purchase anxiety rates in e-commerce. UGC, before-and-after imagery, and verified review counts are the primary trust drivers — more influential than brand authority alone.
NotebookLM · Boom by Cindy Joseph, Founders Formula audit
BNPL near the price point increases AOV
Adding a "4 payments of $X" instalment display directly adjacent to the product price — not buried in checkout — measurably increases conversion and average order value on products above $80 AUD.
NotebookLM · Founders Formula CRO study
Shipping anxiety is a leading cart abandonment trigger
Unexpected or uncertain shipping costs and delivery timelines are cited in 48–58% of cart abandonment surveys. Proactive, specific delivery estimates ("Ships in 2–3 days") outperform vague reassurances.
NotebookLM · E-commerce cart abandonment research
Skin concern filtering drives faster purchase decisions
Shoppers who can filter by their primary concern (Dryness, Aging, Acne) convert 23–35% faster than those forced to browse by product category. Sephora and Clinique usability tests confirm this pattern consistently.
NotebookLM · Sephora, Clinique UX research
Loyalty point clarity drives repeat purchase intent
Displaying exactly how many points a specific purchase earns — and what those points are worth — on the PDP increases repeat purchase intent and account creation. Vague "earn rewards" copy underperforms dollar-specific messaging.

1
BNPL Payment Instalment Display
Cart PDP
I 8 C 6 E 7 ICE 7.0
Insight
Kora's core products are $42–$140 AUD. No Afterpay or BNPL option is shown on the PDP or in the cart at any point before checkout. At these price points, showing an instalment breakdown ("4 × $25.50 with Afterpay") reduces the perceived price barrier and is particularly effective on considered purchases like certified organic skincare.
Hypothesis
If we display a "4 payments of $X with Afterpay" instalment line below the product price on the PDP, and again below the subtotal in the cart, then cart-to-checkout rate and PDP add-to-cart rate will increase — because price anxiety for premium products is reduced when the payment feels manageable.
What customers would see — PDP (above Add to Cart)
Noni Glow Face Oil · 30mL ★★★★★ 4.9 · 1,865 reviews $102.00 or 4 × $25.50 with [Afterpay logo] ⓘ FREQUENCY ◉ One-time purchase · $102 ○ Subscribe: Save 10% · $91.80 ADD TO CART — $102
What customers would see — Cart (above Checkout)
Subtotal $102.00 Pay in 4 × $25.50 with Afterpay · No interest [Afterpay logo] CHECKOUT
Implementation (Shopify)
If Afterpay is already connected via Shopify Payments, the instalment widget is available via Afterpay's on-site messaging JavaScript snippet — no app required. Alternatively, a static "4 × $XX" liquid snippet can be added to the product template using {{ product.price | divided_by: 400.0 | money }}. A/B test via GrowthBook feature flag on PDP and cart templates.
Why We Expect It to Work
Afterpay messaging at the price point — not at checkout — is the highest-converting BNPL placement. Founders Formula audit confirms a direct CVR lift from adding BNPL display before the ATC click. Kora's products are exactly in the $40–$150 sweet spot where BNPL messaging is most effective.
2
Cart Trust Block — Replace Delay Banner
Cart
I 7 C 6 E 8 ICE 7.0
Insight
The current cart leads with a yellow "Shipping Delays" warning banner as the dominant above-fold element. This is the first thing a customer reads before deciding to proceed to checkout. Combined with the absence of trust signals near the CHECKOUT button, the cart currently amplifies anxiety rather than resolving it.
Hypothesis
If we replace the yellow delay warning with an honest but confidence-framing delivery message, and add three trust badges (Secure Checkout, Certified Organic, Free Returns) below the CHECKOUT button, then cart-to-checkout rate will increase — because the checkout experience will feel safe and transparent rather than risky.
Variant B — Cart above-fold redesign
⚠ SHIPPING DELAYS — We're currently transitioning to a new warehouse... 📦 Orders currently dispatching within 5–8 business days. Thank you for your patience while we move to our new warehouse. SUBTOTAL $102.00 or 4 × $25.50 with Afterpay CHECKOUT 🔒 Secure Checkout 🌿 Certified Organic ↩ Easy Returns Shipping & duties calculated at checkout
Implementation (Shopify)
Replace the existing announcement bar in cart.liquid or the cart drawer template. Trust badge strip can be added as a static SVG row or using Shopify's section schema. GrowthBook feature flag targeting cart page only. No app required — pure Liquid/CSS change.
Why We Expect It to Work
Cart abandonment research consistently shows shipping uncertainty as a top-3 exit driver. The current banner doesn't hide the delay — it leads with it in a visually alarming yellow. An honest reframe that provides specifics ("5–8 business days") addresses the concern without amplifying it. Trust badges at the CTA are a standard conversion lift lever across Shopify.

Skin Concern Filters on PLP
PLP
I 6 C 6 E 8 ICE 6.7
Insight
Kora's product metadata already includes detailed skin concern tags: Dryness, Dullness + Uneven Texture, Lines + Wrinkles, Sensitivity + Redness, Uneven Tone + Dark Spots, Aging Protection. These are unused as collection filters. The current sidebar only filters by product type (Moisturizers, Oils, etc.) — the opposite of how skincare shoppers browse.
Hypothesis
If we expose Kora's existing skin concern product tags as filterable facets in the PLP sidebar (above or alongside the current category filters), then time-to-add-to-cart will decrease and browse-to-PDP click-through rate will increase — because shoppers can self-select into the right product category based on their primary skin concern.
PLP Sidebar — Variant B
Sort By BESTSELLING ▾ Skin Concern □ Dryness □ Dullness + Uneven Texture □ Lines + Wrinkles □ Sensitivity + Redness □ Uneven Tone + Dark Spots □ Aging Protection Category □ Cleansers + Mists □ Serums + Treatments □ Moisturizers □ Oils □ Eye Care □ Exfoliators
Implementation (Shopify)
All required tags already exist in Kora's product catalogue. Add Filter: [Concern] tag format to the Shopify storefront filter configuration in Online Store → Navigation → Search & Discovery. Zero product-level changes required — this is a pure storefront filter configuration update. Test via GrowthBook with a feature flag toggling the filter visibility.
Why We Expect It to Work
Sephora and Clinique usability studies show shoppers who filter by concern convert 23–35% faster. Kora already has the Skin Quiz (which also maps to concerns) — surfacing concern filters on the PLP aligns the browse experience with how customers are already thinking. This is one of the lowest-effort, highest-potential changes on the roadmap given the metadata already exists.

Risk Assessment
Risks and Mitigations
⚠
Risk
BNPL messaging may not match Kora's premium brand positioning — budget payment options can feel incongruent with a certified organic, luxury skincare brand.
Mitigation
Use clean, minimal BNPL presentation without emphasising affordability. Frame as "flexibility" not "budget". Keep styling consistent with KORA's existing design language. Test before committing to site-wide rollout.
📦
Risk
Reframing the shipping delay banner may be seen as dishonest or minimising a real operational issue that customers are already experiencing.
Mitigation
The proposed variant is transparent — it still communicates the delay with specific timeframes, it just removes the alarming yellow styling and leads with a specific estimate. Honesty is preserved; anxiety is not amplified unnecessarily.
🔍
Risk
Adding skin concern filters to the PLP without GA4 data means we don't know which concerns are driving the most traffic — we could surface the wrong filters prominently.
Mitigation
Surface all concern filters equally in alphabetical order for the initial test. Once GA4 is connected, reorder by search volume and click frequency. This is low-risk given the tags already exist.
🛍
Risk
The "Complete Your Ritual" cross-sell could increase page complexity and cause decision paralysis, actually reducing single-product conversion rates.
Mitigation
Place the cross-sell below the fold (after all accordions). Monitor both ATC rate for the primary product AND cross-sell add rate independently. If primary ATC drops, pull the test. Track AOV as the primary success metric, not just CVR.
