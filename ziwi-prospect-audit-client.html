Section 02
Key CRO Findings
Three findings that directly limit retailer click-through rate — the site's sole conversion event. All are addressable without checkout changes or discounting.
● High Severity
The Only Conversion CTA is Visually Buried
The entire site funnels visitors toward one action: clicking a retailer "Buy now" button. Yet this zone is introduced by a long passive sentence ("Looking to buy in store or from your favourite online retailer?") and three low-contrast rows that read as navigation items rather than primary CTAs. The most important element on every PDP is styled like a secondary footer element.
▶ PDP · Where to Buy Zone
● High Severity
Price Completely Hidden — Confident Buyers Left Guessing
Prices are fully populated in Shopify's database ($11.99–$130.32 NZD) but deliberately hidden from both PLP cards and PDP pages. ZIWI's own homepage copy targets "label readers" — price-conscious, ingredient-scrutinising buyers — yet these same customers arrive at retail partners without any price expectation, creating friction and drop-off at the handoff point.
▶ PLP & PDP · Price Display
● Medium Severity
Thin Review Counts Undercut Premium Positioning
Flagship Air-Dried products carry 6–8 reviews. Newer Steam & Dried SKUs show 0–1. For a brand priced at a significant premium, social proof at the moment of purchase intent is critical. Displaying a 2-sentence AI-generated summary of existing reviews adjacent to the "Buy now" buttons would concentrate available trust signals at the exact decision point.
▶ PDP · Social Proof Zone
Section 03
Proposed A/B Tests
Three tests designed for ZIWI's retailer-redirect model. Each targets the retailer click-through event — the equivalent of "Add to Cart" for this site architecture.
Test 01 · PDP · Where to Buy Zone
Redesign the Retailer CTA Zone for Visual Priority
Replacing the passive "Looking to buy..." sentence with a clear action-oriented heading, upgrading retailer buttons to full-width high-contrast cards, and tagging the recommended retailer will lift the retailer click-through rate by giving ZIWI's sole conversion CTA the visual weight it deserves.

Section 05
Recommended Next Steps
Suggested 6-week implementation and testing roadmap, prioritised by effort and impact.
Week 1–2
Instrument & Baseline
Set up GrowthBook SDK, add event tracking for retailer "Buy now" button clicks (currently untracked in most setups), and confirm current click-through rates per retailer and per product. This baseline is critical — retailer CTR is likely the only metric on the ZIWI Shopify site that maps to revenue.
Week 2
Launch Test 02 — Price Anchoring
Quickest implementation: a single Liquid price filter on the product card and PDP header. Can ship and reach statistical significance within 7–10 days. The result will also validate the price transparency hypothesis before investing in Test 01.
Week 3
Launch Test 01 — Retailer CTA Redesign
4–6 hours of HTML/CSS work to transform the retailer zone from a passive set of rows into a high-priority purchase destination. Expected to be the highest-impact test — run 50/50 on all PDP traffic with retailer CTR as the primary metric.
Week 4
Launch Test 03 — AI Review Summary
Generate per-product review summaries (or product-specific benefit statements for 0-review SKUs). Load as Shopify metafields. Render above the retailer buttons. Run 50/50 on PDP traffic — stack this test once Tests 01 and 02 have reached significance.
Week 5–6
Read Results & Stage 2 Planning
Ship winners to 100% of traffic. Stage 2 audit to explore deeper opportunities: international retailer expansion (adding AU/US retail partners per market), "Build your bowl" conversion optimisation, and homepage hero CTA testing to drive more PDP visits.
