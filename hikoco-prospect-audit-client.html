Hypothesis

If we add a trust badge cluster (🔒 Secure Checkout · 🇳🇿 NZ-Based · ↩ Easy Returns) directly above the "Check out" CTA and insert 2–3 "You may also like" product tiles below the cart summary, then cart-to-checkout rate and AOV will both increase — because beauty shoppers experience peak purchase anxiety at checkout and need explicit reassurance, while product recommendations capitalise on the highest-intent moment in the funnel.

Mechanism

Purchase anxiety is highest when a shopper is about to commit. Trust signals reduce perceived risk. For a Korean beauty brand selling to NZ shoppers, localised signals (NZ-based, easy returns, secure checkout) are particularly effective. Cross-sell tiles in the cart leverage the "foot-in-the-door" effect — shoppers who've already decided to buy are the most receptive to complementary recommendations.

Implementation

Add a trust badge row to the Shopify cart template (cart.liquid) immediately above the checkout button — three inline pills with icon + short text. Below the cart items, inject a "Complete your routine" section using a featured collection or metafield-driven cross-sell logic. Wrap in a GrowthBook feature flag for clean A/B split.

Test 02 · PDP
Sticky Add-to-Cart Bar
PDP ATC Rate Mobile-First
7 Impact
8 Conf.
7 Ease

Hypothesis

If we implement a sticky bottom bar that appears once the static "Add to cart" button scrolls out of view — showing the product name, price, and a prominent CTA — then add-to-cart rate will increase because Korean beauty shoppers are ingredient-conscious researchers who scroll deep into product descriptions, "How to use" and reviews before buying, and currently must scroll back to the top to convert.

Mechanism

Hikoco's PDP structure includes multiple accordion sections (How to Use, Ingredients, Beauties' Say) that encourage deep scrolling. Shoppers who reach the bottom of the page with high purchase intent have no persistent buying mechanism — the friction of scrolling back up is enough to cause drop-off, especially on mobile. A sticky bar eliminates this micro-friction entirely.

Implementation

Inject a sticky footer bar via JavaScript into the PDP template (product.liquid). Bar appears when the main ATC button's bounding rect top exceeds the viewport height. Contains: thumbnail, product name (truncated), price, and "Add to cart" button. On mobile, occupies the bottom safe zone. Wrap in GrowthBook flag pdp-sticky-atc-v1.

Expected Lift & Feature Flag

Add-to-Cart Rate: +3–6%
Mobile ATC Rate: +5–9% (mobile impact typically higher)
Feature flag: pdp-sticky-atc-v1
Runtime: 3–4 weeks

Star Rating Chips on Product Cards

Hypothesis

If we display actual Judge.me star ratings and review counts on every product card across collection pages, then PLP-to-PDP click-through rate will increase because shoppers browsing 1,215+ products use ratings as a primary filter signal — and currently every card appears equally unvalidated, creating decision paralysis and suppressing click depth. Top-rated products like Beauty of Joseon (4.80★, 586 reviews) will see outsized lifts.

Mechanism

This is the highest-confidence test in the audit — the evidence is direct and the fix is architectural. Judge.me is already installed and firing correctly on PDPs. The collection page product card template simply isn't rendering the widget. With 39,293 total reviews across the catalogue, Hikoco has more social proof than most beauty brands — it just isn't surfaced where browse decisions are made.

Implementation

Add the Judge.me collection widget snippet to the product card template (product-card.liquid or equivalent in the Fetch theme). Configure the widget to display inline star chips with review count. For products with zero reviews, show no widget (not 0 stars). Enable via GrowthBook flag plp-star-chips-v1 to measure incremental CTR lift cleanly.

Why This Works

The psychology behind each test

🔒

Purchase Anxiety Peaks at Checkout

The moment before clicking "Check out" is when doubt spikes. Trust signals at that exact moment — secure checkout, local fulfilment, easy returns — directly reduce abandonment by addressing the "what if something goes wrong" fear.

⭐

Social Proof is a Beauty Category Superpower

Beauty shoppers are risk-averse when trying new products — will this break me out? Is this worth the money? Star ratings answer both questions instantly. For a multi-brand retailer with 1,215+ SKUs, ratings are the primary navigation shortcut available at the browse stage.

📍

The Sticky ATC Eliminates the Most Common Drop Point

On a beauty PDP, the scroll journey from ATC button to ingredient list to reviews is routinely 3–5 screen heights. Every shopper who reaches the bottom has shown deep engagement — the sticky bar converts that engagement into action without friction.

🛒

Cart Cross-sells Hit the Highest-Intent Moment

Shoppers in the cart have already committed psychologically. They're in "buy mode." Relevant complementary products shown here (tonerthat pairs with a cleanser, SPF that goes with a serum) see click-to-purchase rates 3–4× higher than homepage or PLP recommendations.

🧪

K-Beauty Shoppers Are Research-Driven

The Korean beauty community skews toward ingredient-conscious, research-first shoppers who cross-reference products before buying. This means longer PDP sessions, deeper scroll depth, and a genuine need for a persistent ATC that survives the research phase.

📈

Bottom-of-Funnel First Means Faster Returns

The cart and PDP already have traffic. Tests here don't need to grow the audience — they convert the audience that already exists. Every percentage point gained at the bottom of the funnel has direct, immediate revenue impact with no ad spend required.

Risks & Mitigations

What we'll watch for

Trust Badge Design Fit
Medium Risk

Generic trust badges can look inconsistent with Hikoco's playful Korean beauty aesthetic and undermine brand credibility if not styled carefully.

Design badges to match Hikoco's colour palette and typography. Use illustrated or emoji-style icons rather than generic stock badges. A/B test with two badge style variants if needed.

Sticky ATC Mobile Performance
Medium Risk

A poorly implemented sticky bar can trigger Cumulative Layout Shift (CLS), reduce Core Web Vitals scores, or cover important content on smaller screens.

Add position: fixed with a bottom safe zone and ensure the bar only activates via scroll observation API. Run a Lighthouse CLS check before launching to production.

Zero-Review Products on PLP
Low Risk

Showing "0 reviews" on newer products could create a two-tier appearance on the collection page, potentially stigmatising newly launched SKUs.

Configure Judge.me to only render the widget when review count is ≥ 1. Products with no reviews get no chip displayed — clean and neutral, consistent with best practice.

Cross-sell Relevance
Medium Risk

Generic "You may also like" recommendations in the cart that don't match the category of the item in cart can feel irrelevant and add cognitive noise rather than driving AOV.

Use rule-based cross-sells (skincare → skincare, makeup → makeup) or implement a complementary product tag logic. Monitor cart-to-checkout rate carefully to ensure cross-sells don't distract from checkout.
