Section 01

Funnel Audit Summary

Mobile-first crawl of odyssey-marriage.com. The booking funnel maps across four stages: Homepage → Solutions → Booking Form → Post-Submit. Key friction identified at every stage.

Stage 1

Homepage

Four competing CTAs across the page ("Schedule a call", "Learn more", "Start now", "Inquire") create decision paralysis and dilute click intent.
Social proof stats (3,400+ clients, 84% success rate) are buried — not visible above the fold on mobile where most visitors land.
No urgency or availability signals. Nothing motivates action now over later — critical for an audience already in emotional delay mode.
Stage 2

Solutions Page

Zero pricing transparency across all three service tiers. No investment range shown — creates a qualification barrier that requires a call just to learn cost.
"Inquire" CTA links to "#" — a likely dead link or unrendered modal on mobile. High-intent visitors hit a dead end.
No trust signals (client stats, media logos, testimonial chips) placed near the service tier CTAs where conversion decisions are made.
Stage 3

Booking Form

Bare inquiry form with no trust reinforcement nearby. Submitting personal details about a marriage crisis requires high trust — none is provided at the moment of commitment.
No process expectation-setter. Visitors don't know what happens after clicking "Submit" — uncertainty is a known abandonment driver for sensitive service forms.
No visible confidentiality statement adjacent to the form. Privacy assurance is especially critical for men disclosing relationship problems.
Stage 4

Post-Submit

Generic success message ("Thank you! Your submission has been received!") with no dedicated confirmation page, no next-step timeline, and no set expectations.
No tier-upgrade pathway after inquiry. Men who submit a Basic inquiry have no prompt to consider Odyssey Plus — a missed upsell moment.
Onboarding email sequence exists. Session link, calendar invite, and intake form are sent post-submission — a solid foundation to build on.
Section 02

Key Insights

Three conversion-critical patterns identified across the funnel that explain why qualified visitors are leaving without booking.

4×

CTA Fragmentation

Four different CTA labels compete across the homepage (Schedule a call, Learn more, Start now, Inquire). Hick's Law shows that doubling the number of choices reduces decision speed and can lower conversion rates by up to 20–30%. For men already emotionally overwhelmed by a marriage crisis, friction compounds fast.

0

Trust at the Conversion Point

The booking form — the single highest-stakes moment on the site — has zero supporting trust signals. No client count, no success rate, no confidentiality statement, no testimonial. This is the equivalent of a checkout page with no trust badges. For a sensitive, high-anxiety service, trust proximity to the form is non-negotiable.

$0

Pricing Opacity Creates Anxiety

The Solutions page shows three tiers with no investment range. Visitors must book a discovery call just to find out if they can afford the service — adding a barrier at the exact moment they're evaluating commitment. Even a "Starting from $X" anchor dramatically reduces pre-call anxiety and attracts better-qualified leads.

Section 07

Why This Works

The psychology behind why these tests drive bookings for a high-sensitivity service targeting men in emotional distress.

🛡️

High-Anxiety Purchases Require Trust Proximity

For any service involving personal disclosure — marriage, mental health, legal — trust signals must appear at the exact moment of commitment. Placing them on other pages doesn't transfer. The form is where doubt peaks, so that's where reassurance must live.

🧠

Masculine Buying Psychology Responds to Data

The Odyssey audience — men seeking structured, results-driven support — is predisposed to respond to concrete outcomes data. "3,400+ clients served" and "84% success rate" align with how this audience evaluates decisions: evidence-first, not emotion-first.

🔀

Hick's Law: More Choices = Fewer Decisions

With four CTAs on the homepage, visitors spend cognitive energy deciding which action to take rather than acting. Reducing to one clear CTA removes the meta-decision entirely. This is particularly powerful for a user already emotionally drained from a relationship crisis.

💰

Pricing Opacity Repels, Anchoring Attracts

Visitors with zero pricing context imagine worst-case costs. A "starting from" anchor resets that assumption downward and allows self-qualification — ensuring that those who do book are already prepared for the investment, dramatically improving show-up rates and close rates.

⏱️

Process Clarity Reduces Submission Anxiety

A "what happens next" micro-section (3 steps: submit → email within 24h → session booked) eliminates the most common reason service forms are abandoned: "I don't know what I'm getting into." Transparency about process converts hesitation into action.

⚡

Availability Signals Create Real Urgency

Odyssey already discloses that 37 men are currently active in the program. That's genuine scarcity data — not manufactured FOMO. Surfacing this near the primary CTA gives visitors a real reason to act now rather than "think about it," which for a distressed audience often means never returning.

Section 08

Risks & Mitigations

Known risks for each proposed test and how to pre-empt them.

Pricing Disclosure Risk
Medium

Showing even a "starting from" price may cause price-sensitive visitors to self-exclude before speaking to the team, reducing raw inquiry volume even if lead quality improves.

Use "starting from $X" framing rather than fixed prices. Position the figure immediately below a strong outcome stat (e.g., "84% success rate — Investment: from $X"). The anchor normalises the price relative to the value of saving a marriage. Track both inquiry volume and show-up quality to assess net effect.

Trust Block Feels Salesy
Low

A cluster of stats and a testimonial above the booking form could read as pushy or performative, especially for a sensitive, trust-dependent service.

Use specific, verifiable figures (3,400 not "thousands+") and real first-name testimonials with location. Avoid superlatives. Keep the trust block visually minimal — chips, not banners. The specificity of the data is what makes it feel authentic rather than marketing copy.

CTA Consolidation Reduces Exploration
Low

Removing "Learn more" and "Explore solutions" from the hero may reduce Solutions page traffic from visitors who aren't yet ready to book, potentially thinning the mid-funnel.

Retain "Explore our solutions ↓" as a secondary text link below the primary CTA — not a button, just an anchor link. This preserves discovery without competing visually with the primary action. Monitor Solutions page traffic as a secondary metric during the test.

Section 10

Next Steps

Recommended actions to move from audit to running tests within 2–3 weeks.

1

Connect Analytics & Set Baselines Week 1

Verify GA4 (or equivalent) is firing correctly on the inquiry form. Confirm funnel events: page views, CTA clicks, form_start, form_submit. These baselines are essential for measuring test lift accurately. Without them, we're running blind.

2

Fix the Broken "Inquire" CTA Day 1 — No Test Required

The "Inquire" button on the Solutions page links to "#" — a dead end on mobile. This is a zero-effort fix that removes a known conversion blocker immediately. Point it to the booking form or contact page. Do not A/B test a broken element.

3

Launch Test 01: Form Trust Stack Week 1–2

Highest priority and lowest risk. Add trust chips, a testimonial snippet, and the "what happens next" 3-step bar above the inquiry form. Run for 2–4 weeks or until statistical significance is reached. Primary metric: form submission rate.

4

Coordinate Pricing Anchor Copy Week 2

Before launching Test 02, confirm the "starting from $X" investment figure with your team. Even a directional range (e.g., "Investment: from $150") is more effective than opacity. This conversation often surfaces important positioning decisions worth making explicit.

5

Launch Tests 02 & 03 Week 3–4

Roll out Solutions pricing anchor and homepage CTA consolidation. If traffic volume is sufficient, these can run simultaneously. If not, sequence: Test 02 first (mid-funnel impact), then Test 03. Review combined performance at the 30-day mark.
